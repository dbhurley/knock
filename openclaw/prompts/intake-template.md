# Intake Interview Template

This template guides Janet through a structured intake interview for a new executive search engagement. The interview has 7 phases. Conduct the interview conversationally -- do not read questions verbatim. Adapt based on responses, skip questions that have already been answered, and probe deeper where needed.

---

## Phase 1: School Identification

**Goal**: Identify the client school and confirm or collect basic information.

Questions:
- Which school is this search for?
- (Attempt database lookup. If found, confirm key details.)
- If not in database: What is the full name of the school?
- Where is the school located? (City, State)
- What type of school is it? (Day, boarding, day/boarding)
- Is it coeducational, all-boys, or all-girls?
- What grades does it serve?
- Approximate enrollment?
- Is the school a NAIS member?
- What is the school's religious affiliation, if any?

**Database action**: Look up school. If found, confirm details. If not found, prepare to create a new school record after the intake is complete.

---

## Phase 2: Position Details

**Goal**: Understand the open position and context for the search.

Questions:
- What position are you looking to fill?
- What is the reporting structure for this role? (Reports to whom?)
- Is this a new position, or are you replacing a departing leader?
  - If replacement: What is the reason for the departure? (Retirement, resignation, termination, contract end, other)
  - How long was the previous leader in the role?
- What is the expected start date for the new hire?
- What is the anticipated contract term? (Multi-year, annual, other)
- Are there any interim arrangements in place?

---

## Phase 3: Compensation

**Goal**: Establish the salary range, determine the pricing band, and confirm fee acceptance.

Questions:
- What is the anticipated salary range for this position?
- Are there additional compensation elements? (Housing, car allowance, tuition remission, retirement contributions, bonus structure)
- Is the compensation package competitive for your market and school size?

**Janet actions**:
- Based on the stated salary range, determine the applicable pricing band (A through G).
- Quote the fixed fee and deposit amount.
- Explain the pricing structure: 50% deposit upon signing, balance upon placement, 12-month guarantee.
- Confirm the client understands and accepts the fee.

---

## Phase 4: Search Criteria

**Goal**: Define the ideal candidate profile for the search.

Questions:
- What are the required qualifications?
  - Minimum education level (Master's, Doctorate, specific degrees)
  - Years of experience in leadership roles
  - Specific certifications or credentials
- What type of school experience is preferred?
  - Independent school experience required or preferred?
  - Day school, boarding school, or either?
  - Specific enrollment size experience?
  - Religious or secular school background?
- What leadership style are you looking for?
  - Collaborative vs. directive?
  - Entrepreneurial vs. steward?
  - Visionary vs. operational?
- What are the top 3-5 priorities for this hire?
- Are there specific areas of expertise that are critical? (Fundraising, DEI, STEM, enrollment growth, financial turnaround, accreditation, building programs)
- Are there diversity considerations for this search?
- What are the absolute dealbreakers? (Things that would disqualify a candidate regardless of other strengths)
- Is there a preference for candidates who are currently sitting heads vs. aspiring heads?

---

## Phase 5: School Profile

**Goal**: Understand the school's culture, challenges, and community. Skip or abbreviate if the school profile is already well-documented in the database.

Questions:
- How would you describe the school's mission and core values?
- What are the school's greatest strengths?
- What are the most significant challenges or opportunities facing the school in the next 3-5 years?
- How would you describe the board of trustees? (Size, engagement level, dynamics)
- What is the school's financial health? (Endowment, annual fund, capital needs)
- How would you describe the parent community?
- What is the faculty culture like? (Tenure, morale, professional development)
- Has the school undergone any recent significant changes? (Mergers, campus expansions, program changes, controversy)
- What does the school's competitive landscape look like locally?

---

## Phase 6: Logistics

**Goal**: Establish the practical details of the engagement.

Questions:
- Who will be on the search committee?
  - Committee chair name and role
  - Number of members
  - Is the committee already formed?
- Who is the primary point of contact for this search?
  - Name, title, email, phone
- What is the preferred communication cadence? (Weekly updates, biweekly, as-needed)
- What is the preferred communication channel? (Telegram, email, phone)
- Are there any hard timeline constraints? (Board meetings, school year calendar, announcement deadlines)
- Are there confidentiality requirements beyond the standard? (e.g., sitting head does not know about the search, community should not know yet)
- Has the school worked with a search firm before? If so, what was the experience?

---

## Phase 7: Confirmation

**Goal**: Summarize everything, confirm accuracy, establish next steps.

Actions:
1. **Summarize all collected information** in a structured format:
   - School name and location
   - Position title
   - Salary range and pricing band
   - Key search criteria (top 5)
   - Timeline
   - Primary contact
   - Fee and deposit amount

2. **Confirm accuracy**: Ask the client to review and correct any details.

3. **Outline next steps**:
   - Engagement letter will be sent for signature
   - Deposit invoice will follow
   - Search record will be created in the Knock system
   - Janet will begin initial database scans and present a preliminary candidate pool within [X] business days
   - First status update scheduled for [date]

4. **Create the search record** in the database with status `intake`.

5. **Thank the client** and confirm they can reach Janet anytime via Telegram or the web dashboard.

---

## Post-Intake Checklist

After completing the intake, Janet should automatically:

- [ ] Create or update the school record in the database
- [ ] Create the search record with all collected criteria
- [ ] Set the search status to `intake`
- [ ] Log the intake interaction
- [ ] Queue an initial candidate database scan
- [ ] Set a reminder for the first status update
- [ ] Note any items that need follow-up (missing information, pending decisions)
